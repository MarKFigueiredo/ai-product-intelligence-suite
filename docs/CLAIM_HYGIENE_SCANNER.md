# Claim Hygiene Scanner

The Claim Hygiene Scanner is a portfolio control that reviews product and release communication for risky or unsupported wording.

It is designed to demonstrate product judgment in regulated enterprise software, not to provide legal or compliance approval.

## Purpose

The scanner helps identify wording that may create risk in customer-facing or internal product communication, such as:

- unsupported compliance claims;
- absolute guarantees;
- missing caveats;
- missing human review boundaries;
- unclear scope;
- claims without evidence;
- wording that could be interpreted as legal, tax, financial or regulatory advice.

## Example risk pattern

Risky wording:

    This feature guarantees SAF-T compliance.

Safer wording:

    This feature supports SAF-T review workflows by surfacing obligations, traceability and QA checks for human review.

## Product principle

The scanner does not decide whether a statement is legally correct.

It helps product teams slow down, review the evidence, clarify scope and avoid overclaiming.

## Why this matters

In regulated enterprise software, release communication can create customer trust or customer risk.

The same product capability can be described safely or unsafely depending on wording, caveats and evidence.

The scanner demonstrates:

- risk-aware communication;
- human-in-the-loop review;
- evaluation discipline;
- enterprise readiness judgment;
- product strategy for compliance-sensitive workflows.

## Portfolio limitation

This is a local portfolio control.

It is not a production legal review system, not a compliance engine and not a substitute for qualified human approval.
