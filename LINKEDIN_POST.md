# LinkedIn Post — Claim Hygiene Version

One of the most useful critiques I received on this portfolio project was not about adding more AI features. It was about overclaim risk.

An earlier version included self-scoring language that could be misread as validation. I removed it — but more importantly, I built a claim hygiene scanner so the same pattern cannot quietly reappear.

That became one of the main product lessons of the project:

> Responsible AI product work is not only about generating better outputs. It is about building systems that prevent misleading outputs from becoming product claims.

The latest version also adds a validation pack for the citation verifier and a second-domain Swiss QR-Bill case, while keeping advanced modules behind a focused review path.

This is still a portfolio case study, not a commercial product. The point is to demonstrate AI product judgment for regulated enterprise workflows: evidence, review, quality gates, scope discipline and honest limits.

# LinkedIn Post Draft

I built an AI Product Intelligence Suite for compliance-heavy enterprise software.

The goal was not to create another generic AI chatbot. The goal was to explore how AI can support real product workflows where auditability, risk and traceability matter.

The suite has four workflows:

1. **Interpret — Compliance-to-Product Studio**  
   Turns regulatory documents into obligations, product requirements, validation rules, QA test cases and traceability matrices, with RAG, citations and quote-level grounding.

2. **Discover — Product Discovery Studio**  
   Converts feature ideas into assumptions, trade-offs, RICE scoring, Jira-style tickets, Gherkin criteria and QA matrices.

3. **Communicate — Release Readiness Copilot**  
   Generates customer-safe release notes, support notes, claim safety review and approval checklists.

4. **Investigate — Decision Timeline Builder**  
   Reconstructs incidents and escalations with timelines, actor maps, contradiction detection and executive briefs.

My focus is AI for regulated enterprise environments: compliance, product operations, QA, release governance and decision auditability.

I do not believe AI product work is just about speed. In enterprise environments, it is about speed with evidence, trust and human review.
