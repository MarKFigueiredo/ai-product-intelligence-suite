Synthetic regulatory-style sample for demo purposes only.

The invoice export must include a customer tax identifier for each taxable transaction. Records without a customer tax identifier should be blocked before export, unless a documented exception applies.

The invoice date must be present and must not be later than the export generation date. If the invoice date is outside the reporting period, the system should raise a validation warning.

Each invoice line must include a VAT code. VAT codes must be mapped to a valid local tax treatment before the SAF-T export is generated.

The invoice total must equal the sum of line net amounts, tax amounts and approved charges. If totals do not reconcile, the export must be blocked.

The system should keep an audit trail of validation results, including the validation rule, record identifier, timestamp and user who generated the export.

For e-invoicing readiness, the system should maintain a structured mapping between invoice source fields, export fields and validation outcomes.

Exceptions should be reviewed by a compliance user before submission. Automated validations should support human review and should not replace formal compliance/legal judgement.
